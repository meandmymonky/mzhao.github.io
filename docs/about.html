About Me

A little more about me, both in and out of work

EDUCATION AND RESEARCH

I am currently an undergraduate pre-law student in my second year at UC Santa Barbara, majoring in Environmental Studies, Geography, and Philosophy, with a minor in Legal Humanities. Within my fields of study, I have a particular interest in environmental policy, and love exploring how ecological research and innovations intersect with legislation to create feasible and sustainable environmental solutions. In particular, I hope to further explore the ecological processes behind larger ecosystemic regulation, and use those explorations to facilitate actionable legislative decisions pertaining to endangered species policy.

WORK

At the moment, I am seeking employment opportunities in conservation research and environmental policy. Since 2025, I have worked with Ken Hiltner at UCSB as an Undergraduate Research Assistant, using ecocritical perspectives and methods to approach historical and modern media, then exploring the impacts this media has had on our modern environmental perceptions. Outside of UCSB, I have been conducting personal research about the ecological impacts of invasive shrew-species on island locales since 2023, specifically focusing on the Irish Isles and the effects nonnative greater-white-toothed shrews have had on the local ecology.

I’m always looking forward for the opportunity to collaborate on new/existing projects with others. Please feel free reach out if you are interested in connecting, working together, or just to say hi!

INTERESTS AND HOBBIES

“Nature’s creative power is far beyond man’s instinct of destruction.”

- Jules Verne, Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea

Outside of school and work, I love listening to music, playing games, and exploring the outdoors. As an ES major, I am constantly exposed to the fact that our anthropogenic actions have created a persistent underlying threat to our environments. Subsequently, I think it is extremely important – now more than ever – for people to go outside, appreciate our natural landscapes, and see what it is that we need to preserve, protect, and restore. California is a locale that is especially great for this, bolstering with some of the richest diversities of nature, something I am constantly grateful for! Some of my favorite outdoor activities include climbing, hiking, and fishing.

CLIMBING

I started climbing when I came to Santa Barbara. It’s quickly become one of my favorite sports, in part due to the problem-solving physical aspect of it, and in part due to the wonderful community that I’ve found. Within UCSB, I am an officer on the Climbing Team, as well as an employee at the ACC – or Adventure Climbing Center – the on-campus recreational climbing center. Personally, I think that climbing is one of the best ways to both meet new people, and to spend some time outdoors!
